The Idiotic Rise of "Buckets"

Google Ngram frequency of "different buckets" + "into buckets" in English-language books, 1960–2022. Indexed to 1960 = 100.

1963–1966
Barbara Minto joins McKinsey — first female MBA hire. Develops MECE: Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive. The intellectual parent of "buckets."
1976
The nadir. "Different buckets" at 46% of its 1960 frequency. McKinsey jargon hasn't escaped the firm yet.
1985
Minto publishes The Pyramid Principle. MECE goes from internal McKinsey practice to MBA curriculum. The seed is planted.
1996
"Different buckets" crosses its 1960 baseline for the first time. The MBA boom + PowerPoint (Office 97) accelerate the spread. Every corporation now has a consultant alumni on staff.
2000–2010
+136% to +239% above baseline. Consulting vocabulary colonizes tech, nonprofits, media, higher education. Your union rep's boss went to business school.
2022
+168% above 1960 and still rising. Astead Herndon brings literal colored buckets onto a YouTube set and thinks it's charming.

Caveat: "Different buckets" and "into buckets" appear in literal contexts too (hardware, agriculture, chemistry). The ngram captures the word's total frequency, not just corporate usage. But the post-1996 acceleration is real and tracks with documented consulting expansion. The signal is the direction, not the absolute number. Data runs to 2022 (en-2020 corpus — Google's latest fully indexed). Source: Google Books Ngram Viewer.
